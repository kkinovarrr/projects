Maria Salomea Sklodowska-Curie

Great woman of all time

Here's a time line of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - born in Warsaw.
  • 1878 - entered school.
  • 1891 - entered the University of Paris at the Faculty of Natural Sciences
  • 1893 - graduated from university.
  • 1897 - began researching uranium compounds.
  • 1898 - discovers radioactivity
  • 1903 - presented her doctoral dissertation “Research on Radioactive Substances” at the Sorbonne.
  • 1903 - awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics.
  • 1906 - became the first female teacher in the history of the university and France as a whole.
  • 1911 - received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
  • 1914 - made portable x-ray machines.
  • 1934 - died at the age of 66.
